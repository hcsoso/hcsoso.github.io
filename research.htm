Research Projects

Most of my active research projects are about planar metrics. What property does planar metrics have that makes them special? What advantages can we take to construct better and more efficient algorithms? Can we represent planar metrics in succinct forms, while preserving the distances exactly or approximately?

Curves and Graphs on Surfaces

  • Untangling Planar Graphs and Curves by Staying Positive,
    with Santiago Aranguri and Dylan Fridman,
    SODA 2022.
  • Tightening Curves on Surfaces Monotonically with Applications,
    with Arnaud de Mesmay,
    SODA 2020.

Efficient Optimization in Euclidean Spaces

  • Deterministic, Near-Linear ε-Approximation Algorithm for Geometric Bipartite Matching,
    with Pankaj Agarwal, Sharath Raghvendra and Allen Xiao,
    STOC 2022.
  • Dynamic Geometric Set Cover and Hitting Set,
    with Pankaj Agarwal, Subhash Suri, Allen Xiao, and Jie Xue,
    SoCG 2020.

Distance-Preserving Structures for Planar Metrics

  • Almost-Linear ε-Emulators for Planar Graphs,
    with Robert Krauthgamer and Zihan Tan,
    STOC 2022.
  • Near-Optimal Distance Emulator for Planar Graphs,
    with Paweł Gawrychowski, Shay Mozes, and Oren Weimann,
    ESA 2018.
